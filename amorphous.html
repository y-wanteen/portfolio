AMORPHOUS

OVERVIEW

Amorphous is a short narrative animation about an astronaut who meets an alien cat on an extraterrestrial planet. The 3 minute animation is done in a low-poly style using Autodesk Maya for the modelling and animation, and Adobe AfterEffects and Adobe Premiere Pro for post-production.

TASKS: Environment design, modelling, animation

TOOLS: Autodesk Maya, Adobe Photoshop

TEAM: Nancy Chen, Danielle Ragas, Josephine Ryu

FINAL VIDEO

MODELLING

We eventually decided on four different environments to use and assigned one to each member to create. I was responsible for modelling the cave environment. I spent copious amounts of time on the crystals in the cave â€“ not only on modelling and positioning them in the environment, but also on experimenting with different render materials. We wanted the crystals to have an opaque, reflective surface that also had highlights and colour gradation. This was an effect that could not be achieved with a single material or render passing that the other environments could get away with.

I eventually discovered that the desired crystal material could be created by compositing different render passes. One layer used Maya's default lambert material for the opaque quality while another had highlights and reflections from a glass material. Furthermore, by using the ambient occlusion rendering technique, better shading and depth could be added to the overall environment.

REFLECTION

This was the first time any of us worked in Maya and was a great learning experience for us. As we progressed through the project, we allocated tasks such as rigging the character models and animating walk cycles to teammates that were more comfortable and proficient at doing so. In the end, we were able to bring to life a story containing with the major narrative beats and quirky characters we envisioned.

Working on this project also gave me more insight into 3D modelling overall and how assets for 3D games are modelled, textured, rigged, and animated.
